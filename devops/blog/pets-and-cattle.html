Server Pettiness and Cattleness

NOTE: The pets/cattle distinction was first discussed in https://www.slideshare.net/randybias/architectures-for-open-and-scalable-clouds

One of the key ideas in cloud architecture is how you treat your servers: do you treat them as pets, or as cattle?

In the old pre-cloud days, servers were treated as pets. They are expensive pieces of hardware, carefully configured, and, because of all the time and energy invested in them, given cute names and lovingly cared for. When they get sick, you nurse them back to health.

In the cloud, virtual servers are cheap, at least in terms of the time needed to procure them. They're almost identical to each other. When one gets sick, you shoot it and get a new one.

Virtualization technology and Infrastracture as a Service (IaaS) offerings like Amazon Web Services, Azure and Google Cloud have shielded us from all the complexity of procuring hardware and connecting them to the data center network. With these IaaS and other similar cloud services, provisioning a new server takes just a few minutes.

However, even if booting a new server on AWS EC2 takes only a few minutes, if you do not have an automated way to configure your servers, then it is very likely that you still tend to treat your servers as pets. Remember the adage "If all you have is a hammer, then everything looks like a nail."? The corollary to that in the devops world is that, if you do not have automated provisioning and configuration, then you'll tend to treat your servers and your environments as pets, not cattle. To illustrate: If configuring a new server involves following an eight-page manual to properly set it up as a web server, then scaling up or down becomes a pain point, and you will tend to just over-provision (run more servers than necessary just to be on the safe side) instead of just running with the exact capacity that matches your current load.

Automated configuration will take you to the next level of server cattle-ness. Combined with newer automation tools like Terraform that perform both server provisioning and configuration, you can boot and configure servers or even entire environments with all the associated network and data services in just a few steps and within a matter of minutes.
